Ricardo Grande Izquierdo

About me

I am a Postdoctoral Assistant Professor at the University of Michigan – Ann Arbor and a postdoctoral associate of the Simons Collaboration on Wave Turbulence, working with Zaher Hani. I obtained my PhD from MIT in 2020, under the supervision of Gigliola Staffilani. Starting September 2021, I will be at the École Normale Supérieure working with Isabelle Gallagher and Laure Saint-Raymond.

More about me can be found in my CV.

Address: Office 2842, Department of Mathematics, University of Michigan
Email: grander [at] umich [dot] edu

Research


I am interested in Dispersive PDEs, Kinetic PDEs, Probability and Harmonic Analysis. Here are some of my publications:

  1. Large deviations principle for the cubic NLS equation (with M.A. Garrido, K. M. Kurianski and G. Staffilani), preprint (2021),
    to appear in Commun. Pure Appl. Math
  2. On the nonlinear Dysthe equation (with K. M. Kurianski and G. Staffilani), Nonlinear Analysis 207, 112292 (2021)
  3. Continuum limit for discrete NLS with memory effect, see Chapter 3 of my PhD thesis (2020)
  4. Space-time fractional nonlinear Schrödinger equation, SIAM J. Math. Anal (2019), 51(5), 4172-4212
  5. Equisizable partial sum families (with I. Kovács, K. Kutnar, A. Malnič, L. Martínez and D. Marušič), J Algebr Comb 51, 273–296 (2020)
  6. On embeddings of circulant graphs (with Marston Conder), Electronic Journal of Combinatorics 22 (2015), P2.28
